     Military Encounters and Group Readiness:
     Reports of our Military engagements:

     Assault by the 1st Atlantians army group on the Tyrant city of Jukanta, located in area OV
     of Synisvania: It was already known that Jukanta was protected to an unprecedented degree,
     3 thick walls and a skilled garrison fronted a defense which included 20 catapults and 100
     ballistae. Late in the afternoon, Commander Rover commanded his 1st Atlantians army group
     to execute the attack on the city. Due to the ruthless nature of the Tyrant, the city's
     defenders were determined to fight vigorously against an attack. As the most important
     group of their nation, the troops are visibly bolstered by the presence of the first
     Atlantians's banner! As the attackers swept over the tall woods, the first missles sang
     out. Early in the battle, defending missilers were skilled, and stalled the attack in the
     early going. The Atlantians lines claimed severe casualties upon the city's protectors.
     Atlantians mages dismounted barely past where the defensive missles were falling and began
     an animated spell. Power-4 wizard Prosperity muttered a few words in a foreign tongue, in
     rapid succession were cast many minor phantasms and magical bolts into the ranks of the
     enemy. Power-4 wizard Prosperity began sorcerous incantations in an unknown tongue. A
     massive ball of fire sped toward the main ranks of the enemy and exploded! Power-4 wizard
     Quagmire began sorcerous incantations in an unknown tongue, with chilling ease he fired
     many volleys of killing illusions and waves of deadly cold into the heart of the enemy
     force. Power-4 wizard Quagmire slowly spoke in an arcane language, gestures building to a
     crescendo. A massive ball of fire sped toward the main ranks of the enemy and exploded!
     Power-1 wizard Aeolus while seemingly detached from all else, seemed to draw within
     himself and began to incant. A faintly visible blue shimmering began to surround the
     troops and noticeably lessened the effectiveness of the arrows of the defenders. The
     Atlantians mages caused tremendous casualties upon the wall defenders. In the initial
     minutes of battle, defending missilers were skilled, and stalled the attack in the early
     going. The Atlantians forces caused heavy damage upon the city's wall defenders. Within a
     few hours, the city defenses were obliterated and an unconditional surrender was offered.
     The Atlantians banner now waves nobly in Jukanta! Atlantians casualties were put at 12%.
     All Atlantians leaders survived the battle. Impressing his peers was Shanna, who once
     again has distinguished himself. He is now a Commander. All the Atlantians wizards
     survived the fray. The troops are eager for more victories: morale is up!

     Assault by the 1st Tyrant army group on the Warlock city of Hammerhold, located in area CU
     of Talking Mountains: Paid informants revealed that Hammerhold had a seemingly impregnable
     defense such as the realm had never before seen. When the sun had risen to its highest
     point, Major General Golindar commanded his 1st Tyrant army group to execute the attack on
     the city. As the most important group of their nation, the forces are visibly bolstered by
     the presence of the first Tyrant's banner! As the attackers swept over the open
     battlefield, the first missles sang out. Early in the battle, defending missilers were
     able to still forever some small percent of their antagonists. The Tyrant lines caused
     appreciable damage upon the city's garrison. Tyrant mages dismounted scarcely beyond the
     range of defending bowmen and began an incomprehensible spell. Power-1 wizard Aqua threw
     back his hood and gestured in an animated fashion. Crackling lightning leaped from the
     wizard's fingers and streaked toward the main ranks of the enemy! Power-1 wizard Kalomon
     slowly spoke in an arcane language, gestures building to a crescendo. Crackling lightning
     leaped from the wizard's fingers and streaked toward the main ranks of the enemy! The
     Tyrant mages imposed minor losses upon the garrison. As the attackers approached,
     defending missilers were heartened by their clear success in the first hour, inflicting
     significant casualties on the attackers. The Tyrant forces caused devastating damage upon
     the city's defenders. It readily became clear that the defending troops were of typical
     quality for the region. Within a few hours, the city defenses were obliterated and an
     unconditional surrender was offered. The Tyrant banner now waves nobly in Hammerhold!
     Tyrant casualties were put at 19%. All Tyrant leaders survived the battle. Golindar
     distinguished himself and has been elevated as a Commander. Cirdan distinguished himself
     and has been elevated as a Major General. Yergin demonstrated tactical awareness and has
     been elevated as a Captain Major. In a setback, the Tyrant Power-1 Kalomon died of his
     wounds. The troops are anxious for more victories: morale is up!

     Power-1 Spells for the Tyrant
     Each spellcaster may cast one spell per turn. Spells cast count as orders used against the
     king's influence order limitation. Each spell requires the spell # to be placed in the
     Order # column of the turn input sheet, the group ID of the wizard's group to be placed in
     Column 'A', and the wizard's power-level (eg:'2') or the specific casting wizard's
     initials to be placed in Column 'B'. Other columns may need to be completed as dictated by
     the specific spell description. Spells not properly completed are miscast and have no
     effect. We suggest that you use either power levels, or the first two letters of the
     wizards name, but not both in any given turn in order to avoid any confusion around which
     wizard may or may not be used.


     Name:    Protect Heroes and Wizards               Spell #:   94

      By casting this spell, the wizard seeks to protect all heroes and wizards of his
      group from the black magics of a particular opposing group's spellcasters. This spell
      is always successful, but is only effective against one group and may only be cast
      upon the group in which the wizard is located. It protects all the group's leaders
      and wizards, even against multiple attacks unless dispelled. Put group ID of target
      group in Column "C".


     Name:    Stun Leader                              Spell #:   95

      The wizard casting this spell seeks to render the opposing commander ineffective for
      the duration of a battle. Your group must engage the target group in combat for this
      spell to be effective. Base chance of success is 100% * difference in level of
      ability (general considered equal to power-2 wizard for this purpose). eg: A power-1
      wizard has a 33% {100%*(1/3)} of stunning a marshal. Put group ID of target group in
      Column "C".


     Name:    Lightning Attack                         Spell #:  102

      The lightning attack is the most commonly known offensive battle spell. The spell
      sends dozens of lightning bolts screaming through enemy formations. The base power of
      the attack is dependent upon spellcaster power: 500 points per level. Against
      divisions, the damage is 150%, damage is 200% vs. armies, and 250% vs. army groups.
      Against towns, damage is 125%, against cities, damage is 150%. Your group must battle
      the target group for the spell to be effective. Put group ID of target group in
      Column "C", or if cast against a population center, leave Column "C" blank.


     Name:    Fear                                     Spell #:  107

      By casting this spell the wizard creates illusionary sights, sounds and smells
      intended to terrify enemy soldiers. The effect is a drop of 10% in the morale, and so
      fighting ability, of the enemy troops for the duration of the battle (not a permanent
      effect). Your group must battle the target group for the spell to be effective. Put
      group ID of target group in Column "C". Not effective against population centers.


     Name:    Create Food                              Spell #:  208

      Wizards in Alamaze can create bountiful quantities of food out of almost any organic
      substance. In game terms, a wizard can create food equal to 2000 units * his power
      level (eg: a power-3  wizard creates 6000 food) by casting this spell. Food created
      is added to available quantities on hand. No Columns beyond Column "B" need be
      completed.


     Name:    Learn Artifact Power                     Spell #:  796

      By using this spell, a wizard can learn what powers are stored within an artifact in
      his kingdom's possession. Information learned may be of a general nature, but it will
      be sufficient to enable the king to know best how to utilize the artifact. Artifact
      must be in the same area as the wizard. Put the short name of the artifact in Column
      "C". Only very basic information will be learned.


     Name:    Magic Research                           Spell #:  799

      This is the means by which a spellcaster advances his power. The wizard must be
      located in a kingdom controlled population center in order to perform his research.
      The cost to the controlling king is shown on the setup sheet data (eg: if cost shown
      is 10,000 gold per level, it would cost 20,000 gold to advance from power-1 to
      power-2). No Columns beyond Column "B" need be completed.


     Name:    Raven Familiar                           Spell #:  888

      By using this spell a wizard may order his raven familiar to perform a reconnaisance
      of an area exactly as a first level agent would. The target area must be within a ten
      area range of the spellcaster's group. The results will be reported in the "agents on
      reconnaisance" section of the status update. Put target area in Column "C".
